What is a Sitemap.xml?

                
An XML sitemap acts as a literal directory for search engine spiders. Instead of crawling your site blindly, the sitemap tells them exactly which pages are most important and how often they are updated. For new sites, this is the #1 way to get indexed fast.

            

            

                
SEO Best Practices

                
A sitemap should only include "clean" URLs. Avoid including pages that are redirected, broken (404), or set to "no-index." By providing a clean list of 200-OK pages, you build trust with Google's crawl bots.

            

        


        

            
How do I use this file?

            
Once generated, save the code as sitemap.xml and upload it to your website's root folder. Then, go to Google Search Console and submit the URL (e.g., yoursite.com/sitemap.xml).

        

        

            
Does the "Priority" tag matter?

            
While Google has stated they rely less on priority tags than they used to, it still helps internal site crawlers understand which pages are your "core" content versus "secondary" pages.
