Understanding Base64 Encoding

Base64 is a group of binary-to-text encoding schemes that represent binary data in an ASCII string format. It is widely used when there is a need to encode binary data that needs to be stored and transferred over media that are designed to deal with textual data.

Is this tool secure?

Yes. ToolForge processes all data locally in your browser. Your text or data is never sent to our servers, making it 100% private.

When should I use Base64?

It's perfect for embedding small images in CSS, sending data via APIs, or obfuscating simple strings that shouldn't be read by the naked eye immediately.

Does it support UTF-8?

Absolutely. Our encoder is optimized to handle special characters and emojis that standard Base64 tools often struggle with.

What is the character set?

Base64 uses 64 characters: A-Z, a-z, 0-9, and the "+" and "/" symbols, with "=" used as padding.
