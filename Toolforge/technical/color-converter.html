Modern Web Color Standards

In the early days of the web, developers were limited to simple HEX codes. Today, we have access to high-definition color spaces like OKLCH and P3, which allow for millions of more colors than standard displays can show.

HEX vs RGB vs HSL?

HEX is the most common for CSS files because it's short. RGB is useful when you need to programmatically adjust opacity. HSL is the favorite of designers because it's intuitive to read (Hue, Saturation, Lightness).

What is OKLCH?

Released in modern browsers recently, oklch() is the "Perceptually Uniform" color space. It fixes the bug where yellow looks much brighter than blue even if they have the same lightness value. It is the future of design systems.
