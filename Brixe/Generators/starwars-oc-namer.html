The Phonetics of the Galaxy

Star Wars names follow specific linguistic patterns—often combining sharp consonants with flowing vowels to create names that feel ancient yet futuristic. Our generator uses a deep database of Galactic Basic phonemes, ensuring your character sounds like they belong in a cantina or a Jedi High Council meeting. Whether you need a mono-name like "Rey" or a complex dynastic title, we prioritize lore-accurate syllable structures.

01. Apostrophe Usage In Star Wars, apostrophes often denote specific planetary dialects (e.g., Twi'lek or Rodian). Use them sparingly to signify an Outer Rim origin.
02. Darth Titles Sith names often reference negative concepts (e.g., Sidious/Insidious, Vader/Invader). If you're building a Sith, look for words that imply corruption or power.
03. Two-Part Names Most Core World humans use a First and Last name structure. Scoundrels and Bounty Hunters often use short, punchy mononyms for quick recognition in the underworld.
