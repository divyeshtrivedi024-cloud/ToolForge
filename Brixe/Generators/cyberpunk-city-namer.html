The Anatomy of a Megacity

Cyberpunk cities are more than just locations; they are characters. From the smoke-clogged alleys of "The Sprawl" to the sterile, floating gardens of "Upper Aether," the name must reflect the divide between the 1% and the street. Our generator prioritizes a massive database of industrial terms, Japanese loanwords, and tech-slang to ensure your setting feels lived-in and dangerous.

01. Prefix Dominance Names like "Neo-Kobe" or "Mega-London" signal a city built on the ruins of the old world. Use "New" only if the city is a sterile reconstruction.
02. Compound Suffixes Combining an industry with a landform (e.g., "Steel-Port" or "Data-Bay") creates an immediate sense of the city's primary function in the global economy.
03. The Language of the Future Incorporating "Chiba," "Kuro," or "Shin" reflects the genre's traditional roots in 80s-era Japanese economic dominance.
