Academia of the Arcane

In fantasy literature, a magical school is often the cornerstone of world-building. A name like "Hogwarts" or "Brakebills" suggests a physical location, while "The Unseen University" hints at the nature of its curriculum. Our generator uses an expanded database of architectural terms, flora, fauna, and Latinate suffixes to ensure your school sounds like it has centuries of history and thousands of ghosts.

01. Compound Phonetics Many iconic schools use compound words (e.g., [Plant] + [Body Part/Terrain]) like "Ravenclaw" or "Hollowbrook".
02. Institutional Status Use diverse suffixes like Academy, Institute, Lyceum, Seminary, or Conservatory to define the school's social standing.
03. Hidden Locations Magical schools are often named after where they are concealed—cliffs, valleys, islands, or even higher planes of existence.
