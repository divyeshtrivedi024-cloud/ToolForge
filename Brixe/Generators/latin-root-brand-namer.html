The Weight of Antiquity in Modern Branding

Latin roots carry an inherent "truth" and gravitas that modern slang cannot replicate. Because Latin is the basis for many English, French, Spanish, and Italian words, a Latin-based name feels instantly familiar and trustworthy across global markets. Our generator uses a dictionary of over 300 radices (roots) to construct names that sound like they've existed for centuries, even if you’re launching them today.

01. The 'V' and 'X' Factor Latin words frequently utilize letters like 'V' (originally 'U') and 'X', which modern branding experts consider "high-value" letters for their visual sharpness and memorability.
02. Suffix Evolution Adding suffixes like -ia, -us, -ex, or -is can shift a word from a simple root to a dynamic entity (e.g., Lux becomes Luxis).
03. Semantic Depth A name like "Aurelius" doesn't just sound expensive; it literally stems from the word for gold, giving your brand a hidden layer of meaning for those who know the language.
