The Language of Corporate Domination

Dystopian megacorps thrive on a paradox: their names are either aggressively descriptive or suspiciously vague. In cyberpunk literature, a name like Omni-Consumer Products tells you they own everything, while a name like Arasaka suggests deep-rooted heritage. Our mega-database provides hundreds of Latinate prefixes and brutalist suffixes to help you build a brand that feels large enough to own a government.

01. Sterile Sophistication Using words that sound clean and clinical (e.g., "Aether," "Zenith," "Prism") masks the gritty reality of corporate greed.
02. Compound Coldness Smashing two high-power nouns together (e.g., "NeuroCore," "TerraSync") suggests a seamless integration of technology and nature.
03. Vague Totality The most frightening corporations use single, abstract words like "The Trust," "Unity," or "Foundry" to imply their presence is inescapable.
