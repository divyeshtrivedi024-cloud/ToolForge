The Mechanics of a Lethal Designation

In high-stakes mecha combat like Armored Core VI or BattleTech, a name is a psychological weapon. Tactical designations often use the "Object-Action" framework—pairing a physical noun with a violent verb or descriptor. Our expanded database prioritizes words that sound impactful over comms: short, punchy, and instantly recognizable amidst the sound of autocannon fire.

01. The Numerical Suffix Adding a version number (V.IV, Mk. II, Unit-01) immediately gives your mech a sense of mass-production or specific history within a fleet.
02. Avian & Predator Hooks High-mobility pilots often take names from birds of prey (Kestrel, Merlin) while heavy tanks favor crushing mammals (Mammoth, Rhino, Kodiak).
03. Corporate Coldness For Armored Core vibes, use sterile, Latinate words like "Vesper," "Steel Haze," or "Redshift" to imply a pilot who is a corporate asset first and a human second.
